# Product

## Register

brand

> **Secondary surface**: The Yifa legal pages (privacy policy, terms of service, compliance, data deletion) hosted under `/legal` represent a **product** surface where design serves a personal finance tracking application. These pages follow product register conventions (clarity, trust, compliance-first) while inheriting the brand's visual tokens. When working on legal pages, treat them as product surface; all other pages are brand surface.

## Users

**Primary**: Potential employers, recruiters, and clients evaluating Andra Satria as a senior full-stack developer. They arrive via LinkedIn, GitHub, or direct referral. They spend 30-90 seconds scanning the landing page, then either navigate to experience or leave. The job to be done is rapid credibility assessment.

**Secondary**: Fellow developers reading the blog. They arrive via search or social shares. They spend 2-5 minutes reading, then leave. The job is learning or perspective.

**Tertiary**: Yifa app users accessing legal/compliance pages. They arrive via app links or app store requirements. They need clear, trustworthy policy information. The job is compliance verification.

## Product Purpose

andrasat.com is the professional home of Andra Satria. It exists to:

1. **Establish credibility** at a glance — communicate senior-level expertise through the quality of the craft itself
2. **Showcase capability** — present work history, technical breadth, and depth through a timeline and skill taxonomy
3. **Share thinking** — publish technical and professional writing that demonstrates perspective beyond code
4. **Host compliance** — serve as the legal home for the Yifa personal finance application

## Brand Personality

**Intentional, electric, human.**

- **Intentional**: Every element earns its place. No filler sections, no buzzwords, no decoration for decoration's sake. The site feels considered, not assembled.
- **Electric**: The aero accent against deep licorice creates tension. Code-like signifiers (`// andrasat`, `contact.init()`) inject energy without gimmickry. The interface feels alive, not static.
- **Human**: Warmth through craft, not through softness. The Konami easter egg, the typewriter intro, the conversational copy — these reveal personality. The site feels made by a person, not a template.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient heroes and stock illustration
- Purple-on-dark "AI" aesthetics (violet glows, neon gradients, glassmorphism-as-decoration)
- Developer portfolios that are just lists of technologies with no narrative
- "Modern and clean" as verbal filler — every design choice should be justifiable
- Template-like card grids where every card is identical
- Centered body copy, all-caps section eyebrows, numbered section markers

## Design Principles

1. **Code as aesthetic, not gimmick.** Monospace appears as structural signifiers, not decoration. The developer identity is communicated through the visual language, not stated.

2. **One accent carries the interface.** Aero (#51b4d3) is the single interactive thread. Restraint creates focus.

3. **Motion serves clarity.** Animations introduce content, confirm interactions, and guide attention. Nothing moves without purpose.

4. **Dark is the default, not a mode.** The palette is designed from dark out. No light-mode toggle splits the identity.

5. **Craft communicates credibility.** The quality of the interface IS the proof of capability. A senior developer's site should feel senior.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target**: WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance
- **Reduced motion**: All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`
- **Color contrast**: Body text meets 4.5:1 minimum against background; interactive elements have visible focus states
- **Screen readers**: Semantic HTML, descriptive link text (no "click here"), proper heading hierarchy
- **Keyboard navigation**: All interactive elements reachable and operable via keyboard
