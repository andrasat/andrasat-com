# Product

## Register

Brand surface. The portfolio communicates professional identity through clear information and visible proof.

## Users

**Primary**: Potential employers, recruiters, and clients evaluating Andra Satria as a senior full-stack developer. They arrive via LinkedIn, GitHub, or direct referral. They spend 30 to 90 seconds scanning the landing page, then either navigate to experience or leave. The job to be done is rapid credibility assessment.

**Secondary**: Fellow developers reading the blog. They arrive via search or social shares. They spend 2 to 5 minutes reading, then leave. The job is learning or perspective.

## Product Purpose

andrasat.com is the professional home of Andra Satria. It exists to:

1. **Establish credibility** at a glance by making identity, role, and evidence easy to understand.
2. **Showcase capability** through work history, technical breadth, current projects, and a concise capability index.
3. **Share thinking** through technical and professional writing that demonstrates perspective beyond code.

Projects contains one group, **Now Building**. Do not create a **Selected Works** group or invent project claims, metrics, outcomes, or descriptions.

## Personality

**Calm, credible, precise.**

- **Calm**: Neutral surfaces, measured spacing, and restrained interaction keep attention on content.
- **Credible**: The first pass answers who Andra is, what he builds, where he has worked, and where to inspect more.
- **Precise**: Specific labels, factual content, readable chronology, and consistent states replace visual noise.

The site should feel modern and human through judgment, clarity, and craft. Personality must not depend on gimmicks, code-editor imitation, or decorative effects.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient heroes or stock illustration
- Purple-on-dark AI aesthetics, neon glows, and decorative glassmorphism
- Developer portfolios that are only technology lists with no narrative
- Template-like repeated card grids, icon walls, or logo walls
- Terminal styling, typewriter introductions, blinking cursors, and easter eggs
- Centered body copy, all-caps section eyebrows, and repeated numbered markers

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence before decoration.** Lead with identity, role, current work, projects, experience, and contact.
2. **One restrained interaction accent.** Aero is reserved for links, selected states, and interaction feedback.
3. **Readable hierarchy.** Commissioner carries all typography. Weight, size, line height, and spacing establish hierarchy.
4. **Themes are first-class.** System preference sets the initial theme. Explicit Light or Dark choices persist and remain fully designed states.
5. **Motion serves clarity.** Motion may explain state change, spatial orientation, or interaction feedback. Reduced motion keeps content immediately available.
6. **Facts stay factual.** Preserve existing content and dynamic values. Do not invent metrics, testimonials, outcomes, employers, dates, or availability claims.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 Level AA.
- Use semantic landmarks, one clear `h1`, ordered headings, and descriptive link text.
- Make every link, button, menu, and theme control keyboard operable.
- Keep visible focus indicators in Light, Dark, and System-resolved themes.
- Maintain at least 4.5:1 contrast for normal text and 3:1 for large text and required non-text boundaries.
- Do not use color alone for current page, project status, or selected theme.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`; never hide content until motion completes.
